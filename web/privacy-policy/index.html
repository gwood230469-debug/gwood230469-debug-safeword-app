SafeWord ("the app") helps a family circle agree on a shared safe word so they can verify a phone caller is really who they claim to be. This page explains what information the app collects, why, and how you can have it removed.

What we collect

  • Sign-in information — when you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive a unique account identifier and (depending on what you choose to share) your name and email address, used only to identify your account.
  • The name you enter in the app ("What should we call you?"), shown to your family circle.
  • Family circle information — the names and (optional) phone numbers of people you add to your circle. Phone numbers are used only to let you tap-to-call that person from within the app; we never use them to send messages ourselves.
  • Your safe word — but only as a one-way hash. The actual word or phrase you choose is never stored in a form that can be read back, by us or anyone else with database access. We only store enough to confirm one has been set, not what it is.
  • Usage of the "loop in" feature — a timestamp and which circle member triggered it, so the person you loop in can see the request. We don't record call audio or contents.
  • A push notification token, if you allow notifications, used only to deliver the "loop in" alert to your device.

What we don't do

  • We do not sell your information to anyone.
  • We do not use your data for advertising.
  • We do not read or store your safe word in a recoverable form — verifying a caller is something you and your family judge yourselves by ear, not something the app checks for you.

Who else sees this data

Your data is stored with Supabase, our database provider, and (only for delivering the "loop in" notification) Expo's push notification service. Neither has any other use for it beyond running the app on our behalf.

How long we keep it

We keep your information for as long as your account exists. You can request deletion at any time — see below.

Requesting deletion of your data

Children

SafeWord is not directed at children and is not knowingly used to collect information from anyone under 13.
